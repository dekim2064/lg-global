Making better use of available
resources

LG is working to improve its products' performance and convenience while minimizing volume and weight through the development of materials and structures. We reduced the weight of one of our refrigerator models(model: K410MC19E) by 2% in 2020 through a change in materials and increased the use of recycled materials by 6% for ten types of plastic.

We're expanding the use of recycled materials in various products, including washing machines, refrigerators, air conditioners, smartphones, TVs, and monitors, with over 11,000 tons of recycled plastic used in 2020. We're promoting the development of alternative parts and technologies for all products, and carefully measuring the performance of recycled plastics we use.

New Uses of Packaging
Materials

We're committed to minimizing our negative impact on the environment, from design to delivery. We use foam plastic instead of Styrofoam packaging for air conditioning systems and outdoor units, and we've reduced the amount of paper used to pack an outdoor unit from 2,950g to 300g.

Outdoor unit packaging is also collected for washing and recycling, which we expect will reduce around 85 tons of paper and 19 tons of Styrofoam annually. Through this, we're actively promoting sustainability and reducing waste.

Sustainable
Recycling Solutions
Chilseo Recycling Center

The Chilseo recycling center processes a large
number of waste appliances from the western part of
South Gyeongsang Province. It can handle 75
refrigerators, 50 washing machines, and 50 small
appliances and air conditioners per hour. The LGE
Environment Team supervises the center, and all the
resources recycled belong to LGE. In 2021, 85%
of the waste was recycled, and 15% was either burned
or buried in landfills. The legal minimum standard for
refrigerator recycling is 70%, but the center has
achieved a recycling rate of almost 80%.

Process of Recycling

Step One
: Disassemble, Crush, Sort

The Chilseo Recycling Center removes
mechanical and plastic parts from appliances.
Materials are then sorted for recycling. A
patented system automates the process,
increasing efficiency and safety.

Step Two
: Broken Plastic to Neat Pellets

Plastic components are crushed, sorted, and
turned into pellets for use in manufacturing.
This reduces waste and energy required to
create new products.

Step Three
: Transformation Into New Components

The pellets are transferred to another site where
they are melted, poured into molds, and transformed
into plastic components for new LG refrigerator
models.

Step Four
: Rebirth

LG repurposes plastic components from
disassembled products at its Changwon plant,
30 kilometers away, for use in LG refrigerators,
meeting the company's strict quality standards.
