E-waste program
E-waste program for
a sustainable future
LG is committed to collecting e-waste and batteries to promote circularity. Through our easy, convenient e-waste program, you can help us make a positive impact on the environment, inspire others, and create a better future for all.

E-waste program- Global

LG provides collection and processing services for WEEE (Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment) according to regulations and requirements in each country.
We also offer free collections for large WEEE in Korea, and we've expanded our service to 78 regions in 47 countries. We make sure all the WEEE we collect is managed in an environmentally friendly way.

Small e-waste program- Korea

We're making it easier than ever to recycle small-sized electronic waste, which has a relatively low recovery rate compared to large appliances. We're striving to reduce greenhouse gas and carbon emissions by collecting small e-waste through nearby LG Best Shops and Resource Circulation Centers.

Battery recycling program - Korea

We launched a "Battery Return" campaign, encouraging customers to participate in resource recycling by returning discarded batteries used in premium LG wireless vacuum cleaners. We also offer a discount of KRW 10,000 ($8.57) on new batteries. We've even set up collection boxes for discarded batteries at Best Shops and other appliance stores.

Chilseo recycling center

The Chilseo recycling center processes a large number of waste appliances from the western part of South Gyeongsang Province. It can handle 75 refrigerators, 50 washing machines, and 50 small appliances and air conditioners per hour. The LGE Environment Team supervises the center, and all the resources recycled belong to LGE. In 2021, 85% of the waste was recycled, and 15% was either burned or buried in landfills. The legal minimum standard for refrigerator recycling is 70%, but the center has achieved a recycling rate of almost 80%.

Process
of recycling

Step 1
Disassemble, crush, sort

The Chilseo recycling center removes mechanical and plastic parts from appliances. Materials are then sorted for recycling. A patented system automates the process, increasing efficiency and safety.

Step 2
Broken plastic to neat pellets

Plastic components are crushed, sorted, and turned into pellets for use in manufacturing.
This reduces waste and energy required to create new products.

Step 3
Transformation into new components

The pellets are transferred to another site where they are melted, poured into
molds, and transformed into plastic components for new LG refrigerator models.

Step 4
Rebirth

LG repurposes plastic components from disassembled products at its Changwon plant, 30 kilometers away, for use in LG refrigerators, meeting the company's strict quality standards.
